Theme

The main theme for the conference is:
Cross cultural aspects of computing and engineering education.

Topics of interest include, but are not limited to:

  • Computing and engineering education research, methodologies and results.
  • Educational technology, software, and tools.
  • Teaching innovations, best practices, experience sharing in computing and engineering education.
  • Course module design, proficiency assessment, and module cross-accreditation.

Guidelines for papers, posters and demos

The main theme for the conference is:
Cross cultural aspects of computing and engineering education.

Topics of interest include, but are not limited to:

  • Research papers (8-10 pages) must have a clear theoretical basis related to the conference themes. Research papers are mainly evaluated by the relevance of the work and awareness of prior research in the area, evidence of methodological soundness and rigour, and original contribution to knowledge
  • Practice sharing papers (4-6 pages) and Work in progress papers (4 pages) should focus on more widely adopted and valued practices within specific institutions. All submissions should describe original and unpublished work. Practice sharing papers are mainly evaluated by their theoretical basis, extent of evaluation, and potential transferability to other institutions.
  • Poster/demo (2 pages abstract) should present innovative ideas for work in the early stages related to research, teaching practice, or tools. Tools demos should include some hands-on work for participants.
  • Group discussion themes for participants to sign up:
    • Improving student engagement in computing and engineering course modules
    • Collaborative learning in computing and engineering - team and project skills in computing
    • Course design
    • Cross-regional and cross-level recognition and accreditation of course modules
    • Multi institutional collaborative research in the area of attitudes and perceptions of computing and engineering.
    • Graduate careers
  • All authors must register and pay in the conference fee in advance for the paper to be scheduled for presentation. No shows result in the paper being withdrawn and the PDF not being included in our files for upload to the digital library.
  • All papers should follow the IEEE eXpress Conference Publishing formatting guidelines. Templates for submissions can be found at http://www.ieee.org/conferences_events/conferences/publishing/templates.html
